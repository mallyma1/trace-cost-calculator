02 — Proposed notification flow via REX Bot

An early-stage idea discussed between Kunal and Mally — not yet finalised or scoped for implementation. The REX Bot is already live on Render and connected to Telegram. The goal is status-driven notifications that eliminate the informal, inconsistent back-and-forth currently spread across multiple group chats. The bot messages freelancers individually and pings Kunal and Mally in a dedicated ops groupchat — keeping existing freelancer groups clean from operational noise. The bot does not replace those groups.

No reply commands needed from freelancers — all notifications are informational. Status updates are made in the sheet or Retool, which triggers the next message automatically. Retool Workflows supports webhook triggers natively (docs.retool.com/workflows/guides/webhooks) — when we migrate off Sheets, a status change in Retool fires a POST to the REX Bot webhook endpoint directly. The bot does not replace existing freelancer group chats — it complements them by keeping operational pings out of those groups and routing them to the right place.
Why notifications only, no replies: Requiring freelancers to reply with commands adds friction and creates a new failure point. The bot's job is to inform — status updates stay in the source of truth (Sheet or Retool), not in Telegram. Issue statuses are the exception — those surface into the group so the conversation happens naturally, where everyone can see it.

05 — Wallet rotation strategy

This is an important operational consideration independent of the tool decision. We have repeatedly experienced exchange-level blockers — deposit addresses flagged, withdrawals held, accounts temporarily restricted — as a direct consequence of transaction history accumulating on our Chainlabs return addresses.

Why the blockers happen: Our return addresses build up a pattern of high-frequency micro-deposits across dozens of token types from many different exchange sources. To an exchange risk engine, this looks anomalous. The longer a single address is in use, the higher the flag probability. Rotating addresses breaks the pattern and resets our risk profile.

The right rotation cadence will need to be determined based on our actual blocker frequency and volume — there is no fixed rule. What matters is that it becomes a proactive, scheduled operation rather than a reactive scramble when a blocker hits.

06 — Centralised visibility and portfolio separation

We do not have a single place where the team can see what has been sent, what is pending, what each chain has cost us, or when we need to top up a balance. Beyond freelancers, Kunal, Mally, Lucas, and other in-house tracers are all operating in silos — trace activity often mixed in with personal CEX accounts, with no unified view of what is happening across the operation at any moment. This is both an ops problem and an accounting problem.

What centralised visibility unlocks: Preempt top-ups before hitting zero on a chain. Understand which tokens and chains are costing the most. Spot fee anomalies early. Build monthly cost reports per workstream without manual reconciliation. Give leadership an accurate picture of trace funding spend on demand.

Proposed portfolio separation

REX Tracing
Core freelancer trace sends across all 30 active researchers. Primary workstream. Funded and rotated on its own schedule.
Ad Hoc Clients
Separate balance per active client — Chainalysis, Elliptic, Crystal, Addressable. Each isolated for accurate cost recovery and client billing.
OTC
OTC tracing workstream — separate from freelancer REX volume to keep reporting clean.
General Tier
Catch-all for trace sends outside defined workstreams. Can be split further as the operation grows.
